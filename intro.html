IDES 151 This is a User Experience (UX) course This course builds on what you learned last year in the Technical Intensive 1 course related to UX and UI, and will introduce new concepts, tools, and processes that you will for any employment opportunities related to desiging interfaces for software or web. Introduction and Expectations Who are you? Who am I? My career looks like this: Software Sales - Director of Sales - Tidal, a digital agency - iQmetrix - UX Design, Product Design, Product Management, Director of Product Design. What will you learn in this course? Personas / User Research UX Strategy / Goals / Tasks and Content Low and High Fidelity Wireframes, best practices, content layout, responsive design / grid systems. Advanced functionality with Sketch (Symbols) Style Guides / Design Guide / Tiles / Mood board. Advanced functionality with Sketch (Components) Building clickable prototypes (Invision, Sketch) and an overview of CSS and HTML via Inspect Projects Project 1 - User Personas 5% Project 2 - User Journey & Sitmap 5% Project 3 - Clickable Wireframes 20% Project 4 - Design System 30% Project 5 - Prototype 30% Participation - 10% What do I expect you to get out of it This course should prepare you with the fundamental concepts you will need to understand for any design job, whether it be graphic / visual design, web or app design, or product design. The goal is to frame the way you think about problems, and how to solve problems, or get to solutions that meet the needs of the human you are solving for. Concepts vs. aesthetics This course is not primarily about the aesthetics of your websites/digital branding work. This course is about understanding your users, understanding UX best practices including navigation, organizing information, happy paths, and understanding the steps you need to take before you start making beautiful designs and interfaces. That being said, if your beautiful designs negatively impact usability, that will be part of my critiques. What should you expect from me? I will aim to deliver the material in a clear, and informative way. I will share my industry experiences whenever possible. I have a lot of stories. I will be available to answer questions during class, but also outside of class via Slack or Email. What I expect from you. Be on time. This shows that we respect each other. It's important.
