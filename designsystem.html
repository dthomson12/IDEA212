Design Systems

What is it?

A Design System is the single source of truth which groups all the elements that will allow the teams to design, realize and develop a product.

Who is it for?

Not just designer or developers. It is for anyone who is involved in the process of building products, and it ensures that everyone is speaking the same language.

What makes up a design system?

  • Guides: How to use a design system.
  • Styles: Colors, Typography, Voice, Iconography, Accessibility, Illustrations etc.
  • Patterns: Layouts, Processes (Errors), Guides for how your product should behave to provide a consistent and predictable process/flow.
  • Components: A collection of resuable interface items for use by designers / developers across any product or platform

Why are they important?

  • Reduce Design Debt
  • Eliminate, or at least reduce inconsistencies.
  • Efficiency.
  • Ensures that everyone is speaking the same language to reduce misunderstandings.
  • A company's products feel like the company's products. (Consistency)

Examples

  • Shopify - Polaris
  • Salesforce - Lightning
  • Google - Material Design
  • React Library - Ant Design System
  • Apple - Human Interface Guidelines
